Cash Flow

Why profit is not cash

Profit is recognised on delivery or invoicing; cash arrives on payment. That is why a profitable business can run out of cash. Cash flow control shows the real money before a cash gap becomes a problem.

Profit vs cash

Profit reflects obligations and rights; cash reflects today's liquidity. The gap builds up in receivables, inventory and advances.

How we control cash flow

We build the cash flow statement, the payment calendar and a liquidity forecast so you see the cash gap in advance, not on payment day.

7 / 14 / 30-day coverage

We look not only at today's balance but at coverage of obligations over 7, 14 and 30 days — the owner's management horizon.

FAQ

Does high profit guarantee cash?

No. Cash can be locked in receivables and inventory. Cash flow control shows the real money.
